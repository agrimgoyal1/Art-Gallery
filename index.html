Art Portfolio

In the art section, I have primarily showcased my sketches of iconic monuments, including the Taj Mahal, India Gate, Lotus Temple, Akshardham, Red Fort, and more. Through these works, I aim to highlight both the intricacy of architectural beauty and my passion for sketching.



Taj Mahal (1648)

The Taj Mahal in Agra, India, was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1648, It is one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a symbol of eternal love and architectural brilliance.




Akshardham (2005)

The Akshardham Temple in Delhi, India, was inaugurated in 2005 and is dedicated to Bhagwan Swaminarayan. It is a stunning spiritual and cultural complex, reflecting traditional Indian and Hindu architectural styles. The temple is admired for its intricate carvings, grand stone sculptures, and beautiful gardens with water shows.



Gateway of India (1924)

The Gateway of India in Mumbai, India, was built in 1924 to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary. It is a prominent historical monument and tourist attraction, showcasing Indo-Saracenic architectural style. The structure is famous for its majestic arch, seaside location, and cultural significance.

India Gate (1931)

The India Gate in New Delhi, built in 1931, is a war memorial honoring soldiers of World War I. Designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, it is known for its majestic arch and eternal flame (Amar Jawan Jyoti).

Victoria Memorial (1921)

The Victoria Memorial in Kolkata, India, was built in memory of Queen Victoria and completed in 1921. It is a major historical monument and museum, showcasing British colonial architecture with a blend of Mughal elements. The structure is admired for its white marble design, large gardens, and rich collection of artifacts.
